About

What This Course Is

A practical introduction to AI for urban and public-health professionals and students — paired with hands-on activities to bring these tools into your real work.

Practical

You'll actually use the tools, not just hear about them. Every day has hands-on activities.

For your field

Examples and data are drawn from urban and public health, not generic tech demos.

Responsible

Human-in-the-loop throughout: AI assists, you decide. We close the week on responsible use.

Decide

Free vs. Paying for a License

Every activity can be completed on free tiers. A paid plan is optional — here's how to weigh it.

Free is enough

You can finish the whole course without paying. Free tiers cover the activities.

A paid plan (~$20/mo)

ChatGPT Plus, Claude Pro, or Google AI Pro give the best models, higher limits, image generation, and fewer interruptions.

Free-tier limits

Expect usage caps, occasional waits, and sometimes older models. Fine for learning; can slow a long session.

Suggestion: start free; if you hit limits or want the best model, one ~$20 plan for the week is plenty. The $100–250/mo tiers are not needed for this course. We don't endorse a specific vendor — pick one you like. Prices as of mid-2026; check current pricing.

Free & private

We'll Also Try a Local Model

Alongside the cloud tools, we'll run a model entirely on your own computer with Ollama — no account, no cost, and your data never leaves your machine.

Why it matters

  • Free and works offline
  • Private — good for sensitive health data
  • No usage caps

The trade-off

  • Smaller / less capable than frontier cloud models
  • Needs a reasonably modern laptop (8 GB+ RAM)

We'll use this on Day 3, but you'll set it up now so you're ready.

Quick start

Install Ollama + a Model (just enough)

macOS

Download from ollama.com/download (or brew install ollama). Then in Terminal:

ollama pull gemma4:12b

Windows

Download the installer from ollama.com/download/windows and run it. Then in PowerShell:

ollama pull gemma4:12b

Test it: ollama run gemma4:12b "Say hello in one sentence."  On a smaller laptop, try qwen3:4b instead.

That's all you need for the activities. Full model-sizing guidance (and which model fits your laptop) is on the Setup page.
