Computer vision

How Images Are Labeled

Classification

One label for the whole image.

Bounding boxes

Rectangles around objects.

Segmentation

Every pixel labeled by region.

Instance

Each object separated individually.

Labels (annotations) are how supervised vision models learn — and what you produce in today's annotation activity.

See worked examples: COCO dataset explorer · Roboflow annotation examples
Vision models

Vision Models You'll Hear About

Classic CV libraries

OpenCV, scikit-image — filtering, edges, thresholds. Still useful, no deep learning needed.

Detection

YOLO (real-time), DETR (transformer-based), Faster R-CNN — find and box objects.

Segmentation

SAM / SAM 2 (segment anything), Mask R-CNN — pixel-level and instance masks.

Self-supervised features

DINO / DINOv2 — strong general-purpose image features without labels.

Vision-language

CLIP, OWL-ViT — connect images and text; search or label by description (zero-shot).

Urban-health uses

Built-environment audits, pedestrian/vehicle detection, green space, informal settlements.

Many tasks combine these — e.g., YOLO to detect, then SAM to segment. The frontier multimodal chat models can also do much of this from a prompt.

Ethics

Generated Images: Use With Care

Misrepresentation

Fake images passed off as real evidence.

Deepfakes

Impersonation of real people.

Consent

Using real people's likenesses without permission.

Bias

Training data stereotypes show up in outputs.

For research and public communication: disclose AI-generated imagery, and never present synthetic images as documentary evidence.

Background: MIT Detect Fakes · Stanford foundation-model transparency · C2PA content provenance

Video

Video Generation

The leading tools

  • Veo (Google) — realism + native audio
  • Runway — pro control & editing
  • Kling — strong value for iteration
  • Pika — fast, playful short clips

Limitations & cautions

  • Clip length still limited (seconds to ~minutes)
  • Consistency across frames can break
  • Compute-intensive and costly
  • Strong potential for misuse — label AI video

Cautionary tale: OpenAI shut down Sora in April 2026 — ~$1M/day in compute, fading engagement, and copyright/likeness lawsuits. A reminder that AI video is costly and legally fraught; verify and disclose.

Research tool

NotebookLM for Research

What it does

  • Upload PDFs, papers, documents as grounded sources
  • Ask questions answered only from your sources, with citations
  • Generate study guides and summaries
  • Create audio "overviews" of your materials

Why it matters

  • Grounding reduces hallucination — answers tied to your documents
  • Fast literature triage and onboarding to a topic
  • A preview of retrieval-augmented use (Part 4)

Still verify against the original sources — grounding lowers, but doesn't eliminate, errors.

Caveats

Where Data Analysis with AI Goes Wrong

Hallucinated numbers

Plausible-looking statistics that are simply wrong.

Buggy code

Code that runs but computes the wrong thing.

Privacy

Uploads may not be secure or de-identified.

Data quality

Garbage in, garbage out — source data matters.

Bias

Training-data bias shapes results and advice.

Your safeguard

Reproduce results in code; never paste protected data into a public tool.

Part 4

Working with Models

You almost never need to train your own. The skill now is adapting and connecting existing models to your data.
The shift

Don't Build a Model — Build On One

Training from scratch

  • Needs huge labeled datasets & GPUs
  • Months of work and expertise
  • Rarely worth it for most teams now

Building on foundation models

  • Start from a capable pretrained model
  • Adapt with prompts, your data, and tools
  • Days, not months — and far cheaper
Platforms to build on
Amazon SageMaker / BedrockGoogle Vertex AIAzure AI FoundryHugging FaceOpenAI / Anthropic / Google APIs

The old "AI development life cycle" still matters when you truly need a custom model — but for most urban-health work, you'll use and adapt, not build.

Open & local

Open and Local Models

Why run a model locally?

  • Privacy — data never leaves your machine (IRB-friendly)
  • No per-token cost; works offline
  • Reproducible and version-pinned for research

How & what

  • Run with Ollama or LM Studio on a good laptop/GPU
  • Open families: Gemma, Llama, DeepSeek, Qwen
  • Smaller open models now rival last year's frontier

For sensitive health data, a capable local model is often the responsible default — even if it's a notch below the frontier.
